Layer 1: Teal Fluid Tendrils

The organic bioluminescent veins radiating from center — how should we render them?

FROM YOUR MOCKUP

Long flowing teal/cyan tendrils that look like smoke or bioluminescent fluid — they radiate from the center blob outward to the edges, curving organically. They have depth (some in front, some behind the blob). They should react to audio — pulsing, breathing, shifting with the music.

A

Shader-Based — Evolved FBM Tendrils

Modify your existing fbm shader to produce tendril-like shapes instead of the current chrome surface. Use domain warping with directional bias to create flowing vein patterns. Change the color ramp from chrome to teal/cyan. Audio drives warp intensity and flow speed.

Builds directly on your existing shader code. Smooth, infinite, no geometry needed.

B

Particle Trails — GPU Particle System

Thousands of tiny particles following flow-field paths outward from center, rendered as trails/lines. Creates a more organic, fibrous look. Each particle strand = one tendril. Audio modulates flow speed and trail length.

More dynamic and unpredictable. Higher particle count but each is simple.

C

Separate Shader Layer — Dedicated Tendril Plane

Add a second full-screen plane behind the blob with its own shader specifically designed for flowing tendrils. Uses a different noise function (Worley/Voronoi) to create vein-like structures. Completely independent from the blob rendering.

Clean separation of concerns. Can tune tendril look without affecting blob.

My recommendation: Option C — a dedicated tendril plane gives you independent control over the tendrils vs blob, and your scene already composites layers via the EffectComposer. Cleanest architecture for the layer-based approach you described.
